## Design Context

### Users

Two audiences arrive at subtleedge.com with different intent:

**Prospects and partners** land on the homepage or about page to understand what Subtle Edge does. They may be potential clients evaluating a consultancy, investors, or collaborators. They should leave feeling that this is a small, deliberate company that does serious work. The site is a signal of taste, not a sales funnel.

**Assessment participants and compliance reviewers** arrive at the Paper Tiger subpages (paper-tiger, privacy, sms-consent) via direct links. Twilio reviewers need to verify business legitimacy, contact info, and consent flows. Assessment participants may land on the privacy or SMS consent page from a text message. These pages must read as professional, trustworthy, and clearly tied to a real company.

### Brand Personality

**Sophisticated. Mysterious. Minimal.**

Subtle Edge is a boutique. The name is the philosophy: the advantage is in the details others miss. The site should feel like walking into a carefully curated space where every element was chosen, not generated. It should suggest depth without revealing everything. A consultancy and product studio that builds AI/ML and cryptography tools does not need to explain itself loudly.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Restrained, warm, organic. The homepage background image with slow pan animation sets the mood: natural, unhurried, confident. The site should feel like it was designed by hand, not assembled from components.

**Two distinct palettes, one sensibility:**

The Subtle Edge palette (homepage, about) uses earthy neutrals with sage and olive accents. Warm beige ground, near-black text, muted greens. Arial as the body font keeps it unadorned and direct.

The Paper Tiger palette (paper-tiger, privacy, sms-consent) uses warmer sand tones with teal structure and terracotta action colors. Manrope/Sora typography gives it a distinct product identity. These pages are branded as Paper Tiger "by Subtle Edge LLC" and should feel like a product within the family, not a different company.

Both palettes share warmth and restraint. Neither uses blue, purple, or any saturated tech-brand colors. The connection is tonal, not identical.

**Anti-references:** SaaS landing pages. Startup templates. Anything with gradient CTAs, testimonial carousels, or pricing grids. The site should not look like it was built with a page builder or an AI tool.

**References:** The feeling of a well-designed business card. A ceramics studio website. An architecture firm portfolio. Minimal, confident, no wasted space.

**Light mode only.** The warm earth-tone palette is the identity. Dark mode would undermine it.

### Design Principles

1. **Less is the point.** The site is deliberately minimal. Every element present was chosen; every element absent was considered and rejected. Do not add sections, features, or decoration to fill space.

2. **Warmth without softness.** Earth tones and organic textures signal approachability, but the typography and spacing should be precise. This is a serious company that happens to have good taste.

3. **Two brands, one hand.** Subtle Edge and Paper Tiger pages have distinct palettes and typography but should feel like they were designed by the same person. Shared principles: narrow content width (540-640px), three-column footer pattern, generous whitespace, restrained color usage.

4. **Mystery over explanation.** The homepage is intentionally sparse. The about page reveals more but still holds back. The site earns curiosity, not clicks. Do not add "Learn More" buttons, feature grids, or explainer sections unless specifically requested.

5. **Craft in the details.** The email obfuscation, the fade-in animations, the background pan, the SVG logo with its three strata bands. These small choices are the subtle edge. Preserve and refine them.

### Accessibility

Standard WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. The Paper Tiger palette has documented contrast ratios in `/design/tokens.json` in the Paper Tiger repo. The Subtle Edge palette should maintain similar rigor: body text on background must meet 4.5:1 minimum.

### Known Anti-Patterns (audited 2026-04-08)

Two `border-left: 3px solid` instances violate the absolute ban on side-stripe accents:
- `about.css` `.product` block (sage stripe)
- `paper-tiger.css` `.sample` block (burnt sienna stripe)

One borderline stripe: `about.css` `body { border-top: 3px solid }` (sage top-edge).

These should be resolved by replacing with background tints, typographic emphasis, or removing the indicator entirely.

### Typography Note

Subtle Edge pages use Jost (display/structural) + Libre Baskerville (body). The pairing inverts the typical pattern: geometric sans for sparse architectural elements (tagline, location, nav), humanist serif for readable text. Jost is precise and museum-like in letterspaced all-caps; Libre Baskerville is warm and textured against the beige background.
